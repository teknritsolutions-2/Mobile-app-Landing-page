The pre-launch checklist every app team should own

A strong launch page is not just a pretty header. It is a trust system, a story, and a signup machine working together.

Key takeaways

The short version for busy launch teams.

  • Lead with one clear product promise and one primary action.
  • Place proof before the signup ask to reduce hesitation.
  • Make the entire page support a single conversion goal.
  • Follow captured interest with timely nurture messages.

Table of contents

  • Start with a clear product promise
  • Add proof before asking for commitment
  • Build around one conversion goal
  • Finish with follow-up systems

Start with a clear product promise

Your hero section needs to answer what the app does, who it helps, and why now matters. That means a concise headline, one supporting paragraph, and one clear action.

Add proof before asking for commitment

Reduce uncertainty with screenshots, testimonials, metrics, and press mentions. A countdown can help if it supports a real launch milestone.

Build around one conversion goal

Pre-launch sites work best when they ask for one thing: an email address, a beta application, or a demo request.

  • Use one primary CTA style throughout the page.
  • Repeat the CTA after proof-heavy sections.
  • Keep forms short and mobile friendly.

Finish with follow-up systems

Once the email is captured, your nurture flow matters. Send welcome emails, launch updates, and progress milestones that keep users invested until release day.
